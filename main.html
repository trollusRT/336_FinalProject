Progress Entry 01

Establishing the Project Direction

Phase: Research & Persona Development

This week we built our persona. We've made a separate page detailing him and some potential wishes he'd have for a stovetop, and some pain points that he would run into or consider in the design of his cookware. While we initially wanted him to live in an RV and run with the unique challenge of portable and modular stovetops, we had to bring our ideas back to focus on a persona based around apartment life by request of the team in Vancouver.

Progress Entry 02

First Round of Sketch Exploration

Phase: Concept Sketching

This week we kept our persona in mind as we made our sketches. We had a load of ideas for different kinds of stovetops. Junniel's idea for a sort of pullout induction stove shelf thing was in hindsight a little dangerous, but the creative freedom we had at the time was pretty fun before we had to narrow things down to a more realistic perspective. We were encouraged to reconsider our designs.

Progress Entry 03

Refining the Sketches

Phase: Iteration & Revision

This week we got back to the drawing board (literally) for another take of our induction stovetop housing. One problem is that we got a little too ambitious with the controls and shape of the stove when we should draw our focus more towards ease of use and storage. Space efficiency. So we'd stick with a stovetop that'd both serve as temporary storage at the bottom as well as some use at the top using studs to attach a specially designed cutting board made of wood (or silicone)

Progress Entry 04

The Induction Stove

Phase: Prototyping

This week we went down after reviewing our sketches to a staff/graduate kitchen room, where Kenneth showed us the example induction stove we'd use for our final design housing. We were surprised by how bulky it was, and that it also had fans? Either way, we'd have to account for that when we consider our first prototype.

Progress Entry 05

Low-Fidelity Prototype

Phase: Prototyping

Here we started prototyping. Maja made it clear during the SolidSpace orientation that we need to get on finalizing our design fast, or else we won't have a great product at the end if we don't bring it up to her quick enough before the final week. We made it using cardboard and styrene. We found during critique that the walls were too small. We made the mistake of modelling the box to the exact dimensions of the induction stove and not giving a couple centimeters for the size. We'll fix this in our next iteration.
